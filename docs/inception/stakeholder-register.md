## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Purpose

This stakeholder register identifies, analyzes, and provides strategies for managing all stakeholders impacted by or influencing the EDUTrack initiative. It serves as the foundation for effective stakeholder engagement, communication, and change management throughout the project lifecycle.

### 1.2 Stakeholder Engagement Philosophy

We embrace **collaborative, transparent, and inclusive** stakeholder engagement:

- **Early Involvement:** Engage stakeholders from inception through delivery and operations
- **Continuous Communication:** Regular, relevant updates through preferred channels
- **Two-Way Dialogue:** Actively solicit and incorporate stakeholder feedback
- **Value-Driven:** Articulate clear WIIFM (What's In It For Me) for each stakeholder group
- **Data-Informed:** Use metrics to track stakeholder satisfaction and adjust engagement approach

**Compliance & Risk (5):** CISO, Data Protection Officer, Compliance Analyst, Security Analyst, Legal Counsel - Teams ensuring regulatory and security compliance

### Appendix D: Glossary

- **Champion:** Stakeholder who actively advocates for the initiative
- **Hallucination:** AI-generated content that is plausible but factually incorrect
- **MAU (Monthly Active User):** Unique user who engages with platform at least once per month
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** Metric measuring user satisfaction and loyalty (-100 to +100 scale)
- **Power-Interest Matrix:** Stakeholder analysis framework mapping influence vs. interest
- **Skeptic:** Stakeholder who doubts initiative value and may resist
- **SME (Subject Matter Expert):** Employee with deep domain expertise who validates content
- **Supporter:** Stakeholder who agrees with initiative goals but may not actively advocate
- **WIIFM (What's In It For Me):** Value proposition tailored to individual stakeholder
