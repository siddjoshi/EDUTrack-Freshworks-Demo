## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Purpose

This communication plan establishes a comprehensive strategy to ensure all stakeholders receive timely, relevant, and accurate information throughout the EDUTrack initiative lifecycle. It defines what to communicate, to whom, when, how, and by whom.

### 1.2 Communication Objectives

- **Stakeholder Alignment:** Ensure all stakeholders understand project vision, progress, and their role
- **Transparency & Trust:** Maintain open, honest communication about successes, challenges, and decisions
- **Timely Decision-Making:** Provide information when needed to enable fast, informed decisions
- **Change Management:** Prepare, enable, and support stakeholders through organizational change
- **Celebrate Success:** Recognize achievements, milestones, and contributions
- **Risk Mitigation:** Address concerns proactively and manage resistance through dialogue

### 1.3 Communication Principles

- **Clarity:** Simple, jargon-free, actionable messages tailored to audience
- **Consistency:** Aligned messaging across all channels and touchpoints
- **Timeliness:** Right information at the right time (not too early, never too late)
- **Relevance:** Tailored to audience needs, interests, and decision requirements
- **Two-Way:** Encourage feedback, questions, and dialogue (not just broadcast)
- **Transparency:** Open and honest, including about challenges and risks
- **Accessibility:** Available through multiple channels accommodating different preferences

### 3.2 Tool Governance

**Access Management:**
- Slack: Open to project team; stakeholder channels by invitation
- Jira/Confluence: Project team has full access; stakeholders have read access to specific spaces
- PowerBI: Role-based access (executives, managers, PMO)
- SharePoint: Controlled access based on stakeholder group

**Content Moderation:**
- Professional, respectful communication expected on all channels
- No confidential information in public Slack channels
- Email subject lines must be clear and include [EDUTrack] tag
- Confluence pages follow documentation templates and naming conventions

**Archive & Retention:**
- Email: 7-year retention per corporate policy
- Slack: 90-day retention; important discussions captured in Confluence
- Confluence: Permanent retention; versioned documentation
- Meeting recordings: 6-month retention on SharePoint

### 4.2 Meeting Norms

**Before Meeting:**
- Agenda circulated 24 hours in advance with clear objectives
- Pre-read materials shared (dashboards, documents) for informed discussion
- Calendar invites include dial-in info, meeting link, and agenda

**During Meeting:**
- Start and end on time (respect everyone's calendar)
- Follow agenda; parking lot for off-topic items
- Active participation expected; cameras on for video calls
- Decisions documented in real-time with owners and due dates
- Action items captured with RACI assignments

**After Meeting:**
- Minutes documented and distributed within 24 hours
- Action items tracked in Jira or shared action log
- Follow-up on open items before next meeting
- Meeting recordings available for those who couldn't attend
